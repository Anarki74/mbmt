Contest Information

We are pleased to announce that, for the third year, the Montgomery Blair Math Team is hosting the Montgomery Blair Math Tournament (MBMT) on Saturday, April 1st, 2017! MBMT is a free middle school math competition that seeks to excite students’ interests in mathematics and pursuing mathematics beyond the school curriculum.

Logistics

The 3rd annual MBMT will occur in Montgomery Blair HS on April 1st, 2017 from 12:00 pm to 6:00 pm. Our address is 51 University Blvd E, Silver Spring, MD 20901. Please arrive on the University Boulevard side of the school. Pizza and drinks will be provided for students. This is a change from last year. Additionally, snacks will be provided for coaches in the grading room (room 161, a computer lab). Coaches are invited to bring snacks for their own team, although it is not necessary.

Contest

MBMT consists of four rounds: the Subject Tests (of which students take two subjects among Algebra, Geometry, Combinatorics, or Properties of Numbers), the Team Round, the Guts Round, and the Fun Round. Please note that there is no Sprint Round this year. Each Subject Test consists of eight questions on one subject and also lasts thirty minutes. The Team Round contains fifteen questions for all team members to collaboratively solve in half an hour. The Guts Round (something many middle school students have not seen before) is graded live, and requires team members to work together on progressively harder five-problem sets, each of which is worth more points than the previous set. Finally, the Fun Round is an exciting, new round consisting of several separate mini-events, including puzzles, trivia, and an estimation round. All of the rounds, except for the Fun Round, will be split by division.

Based on last year’s results, we have continued to make significant changes to the difficulty of the competition. We have continued to improve the accessibility of problems in Division B, and tweak the difficulty of the harder problems in Division A. We feel that every participant in MBMT should feel successful, while the best students should be challenged at a high level.

Individual and team winners in each division will receive trophies or medals! Team scores are calculated using Team and Guts round scores, combined with a weighted average of individual scores, and teams with the highest scores in each division win. The individual awards are for high-scoring individuals in each subject test and overall, combining the subject test scores. There are also additional awards for high performance on the Guts round. Finally, we also provide the Eric Walstein Award to the highest-scoring 6th grade student.
